First Workshop on Recent Advances in

Fairness in Distributed Applications

(Co-Located with Financial Cryptography and Data Security 2025)

April 18, 2025

Hotel Shigira Mirage

Miyakojima, Japan

Fairness has always been a cornerstone of effective and equitable interactions, and this principle is increasingly crucial in decentralized and distributed systems. The evolving challenges in ensuring fairness within these systems are multifaceted. They include scalability issues, where maintaining fairness becomes complex as systems grow; the integration of fairness with security and privacy, ensuring that fairness mechanisms do not compromise data confidentiality; real-time and adaptive fairness, requiring solutions that can handle dynamic environments; and managing multiple notions of fairness within a single system. Additionally, critical concerns are addressing vulnerabilities related to centralized trust, designing fair incentive structures, and translating theoretical concepts into practical, deployable solutions. Interdisciplinary integration across cryptography, game theory, finance, and distributed systems also presents ongoing challenges.

The FC workshop on Recent Advances in Fairness in Distributed Applications will be dedicated to exploring these critical issues. It will focus on achieving fairness in decentralized systems like blockchains and other distributed applications. This inaugural edition, featuring an invited-speaker-only format, aims to bring together leading researchers from academia and industry to discuss and address these evolving challenges. The goal is to uncover new problem domains, promote cross-disciplinary research, and develop practical solutions with real-world impact. The workshop will mainly focus on the topics listed below:

  • Rational Cryptography
  • Fairness in Multi-party Computation
  • Distributed Key Generation
  • Timed Cryptography
  • Threshold Cryptography
  • Fairness using Trusted Hardware
  • Fairness in Randomness generation
  • Game-theoretic fairness notions - collusion models
  • Fairness in DeFi applications
  • Fair reward distribution in consensus algorithms
  • Fair Payments in Cryptocurrencies
  • Co-operative Games and Fairness Equilibriums
  • Fairness in Digital Auctions
  • Legal and Policy framework for Fairness
